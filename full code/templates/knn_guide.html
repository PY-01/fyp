Welcome to the k-Nearest Neighbours guide!

This page will walk you through using the k-NN algorithm on our platform, from inputting data to adjusting parameters and visualising results.

Steps to Use k-NN

  1. Input your dataset values (coordinates and class labels).
  2. Select the value of k, the number of nearest neighbours to consider.
  3. Click "Run" to see the classified results and visualisation.
    • Buttons:
      • Play: Starts the animation of the k-NN algorithm's classification process.
      • Pause: Pauses the current animation at its current frame, allowing you to observe a specific moment.
      • Forward: Moves the animation one step forward, showing the next frame of the process.
      • Rewind: Moves the animation one step backward, showing the previous frame.
      • End: Jumps to the final frame, displaying the completed classification result.
      • Reset: Resets the animation to the starting frame, allowing you to replay the entire process from the beginning.
    • Radio Buttons (Playback Options):
      • Once: Plays the animation once, stopping automatically at the final frame.
      • Loop: Continuously repeats the animation from the beginning after reaching the end.
      • Reflect: Plays the animation forward and then in reverse, creating a back-and-forth effect.
    • Slider (Animation Speed):
      • Animation Speed: Controls the speed of the animation. Moving the slider to the right increases the speed, while moving it to the left slows down the animation, allowing for a closer look at each step of the process.
